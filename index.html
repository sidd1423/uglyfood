Ugly is the New Beautiful

We started UglyRipe because we couldn't stand watching perfectly good food go to waste. Here's our story.

Our Story

We Produce 1.2x More Food Than We Need

Yet 733 million people face hunger every day. Food prices in Canada are rising at 4.4% annually, outcompeting inflation. Food banks continue hitting new records, with more people relying on them than ever before.

It largely comes down to people's perception of "good food" and how they use it. In Canada, 46.5% of produce is discarded due to strict cosmetic standards imposed by big box grocery stores. In the food industry, 38% of produce is wasted before it ever reaches consumers — visually appealing produce rejected at every step of the supply chain for not looking "right." Once discarded, it does not simply disappear — rotting vegetables and fruits contribute to 10% of total methane emissions.

Strict cosmetic standards have left a gap in the market, throwing away produce that is "unfit" for sale but still perfectly safe for human consumption. 41.7% of alleged "waste" can be redirected, representing a generated revenue of $58 billion dollars. Introducing all of this produce back onto the market can help lower nationwide prices and reduce scarcity in more sparsely populated areas far from major cities.

We target the "Goldilocks Zone" of produce — items that are rejected but remain perfectly edible. By purchasing this produce in bulk at discounted prices and selling it directly to consumers, we reduce food waste, lower grocery costs, and create a more efficient supply chain. Rather than focusing on individual consumer habits, we address waste at the supply chain level, where nearly half the problem exists.

We partner with local farmers, distributors, and suppliers who regularly discard imperfect produce. Incoming produce is inspected, sorted, and packaged into mystery boxes of mixed seasonal items — a flexible model that lets us adapt to seasonality and supply disruptions. Boxes are sold directly to consumers at approximately $1.25–$1.50 per pound, covering all costs with a healthy margin. Our service starts in Toronto, then expands outward as operations stabilize.

As we grow, we will expand beyond direct consumer sales into strategic partnerships with restaurants, schools, and hospitals. We plan to invest in logistics technology and automation to increase efficiency and reduce costs. Long term, UglyRipe can expand past the produce sector entirely. Our vision is a nationwide network that redefines food standards — where produce is judged by quality and nutrition, not appearance.

We start small, then expand from there. Get ready for a journey where we cut prices, reduce waste, and save the planet.
